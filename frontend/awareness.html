Intrusions, Attacks, and What This IDS Does
Intrusions are unauthorized activities on a network. Attacks are the methods used (e.g., DoS, Probe, R2L, U2R, Malware, Phishing).

Common Cyber Threats & Attack Types
DoS/DDoS Attacks
CRITICAL
Denial of Service attacks overwhelm systems with traffic to make services unavailable to legitimate users.
Examples:
  • Volume-based attacks (UDP floods)
  • Protocol attacks (SYN floods)
  • Application layer attacks (HTTP floods)
Prevention: Use DDoS protection services, rate limiting, and traffic filtering.
Probe Attacks
HIGH
Reconnaissance attacks that scan networks to discover vulnerabilities, open ports, and system information.
Examples:
  • Port scanning (Nmap)
  • Vulnerability scanning
  • Network enumeration
Prevention: Use firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and network segmentation.
R2L (Remote to Local)
CRITICAL
Attacks where unauthorized users gain local access to a system from a remote location.
Examples:
  • Password attacks (brute force)
  • Dictionary attacks
  • Social engineering
Prevention: Strong passwords, MFA, account lockout policies, and user training.
Malware Attacks
CRITICAL
Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.
Examples:
  • Ransomware
  • Trojans and backdoors
  • Spyware and keyloggers
Prevention: Antivirus software, regular updates, email filtering, and user awareness.
Phishing Attacks
HIGH
Social engineering attacks that trick users into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.
Examples:
  • Email phishing
  • Spear phishing
  • Whaling (CEO fraud)
Prevention: Email security, user training, and verification procedures.
Insider Threats
MEDIUM
Security risks that come from people within the organization who have authorized access.
Examples:
  • Malicious insiders
  • Negligent employees
  • Compromised accounts
Prevention: Access controls, monitoring, background checks, and training.
Best Practices for Cybersecurity
Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Create complex passwords with at least 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a different password for each account.
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Add an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification beyond just your password.
Keep Software Updated
Regularly update your operating system, applications, and security software to patch known vulnerabilities.
Secure Your Network
Use WPA3 encryption for Wi-Fi, avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities, and consider using a VPN.
Regular Data Backups
Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of important data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy stored offsite.
Stay Vigilant
Be suspicious of unexpected emails, links, or attachments. Verify requests for sensitive information through alternative channels.
Continuous Learning
Stay informed about the latest threats and security practices. Attend cybersecurity training and awareness programs.
Monitor Your Accounts
Regularly check your accounts for suspicious activity and set up alerts for unusual transactions or login attempts.
